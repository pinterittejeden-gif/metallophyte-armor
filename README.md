# Metallophyte-Armor

could a pine move copper from contaminated soil into inner wood that can be monitored and harvested?

that's Metallophyte-Armor. the difficult bit is not simply making a copper-tolerant tree - it's moving the copper without poisoning the plant, then trapping it in the right tissue.

**read the full thing:** [pinterittejeden-gif.github.io/metallophyte-armor](https://pinterittejeden-gif.github.io/metallophyte-armor/)

## where the idea stands

- **what I found:** plants already manage metals, phytoremediation exists, copper preserves wood, and pine tissue changes as sapwood becomes heartwood.
- **my leap:** temporarily buffer copper during transport, then capture more of it near the heartwood transition.
- **the annoying problem:** I still need a believable targeting mechanism and a complete whole-tree copper mass balance.
- **what I'm not claiming:** I haven't engineered the tree, tested the lumber, or shown that it can remediate a site safely.

## what's in here
