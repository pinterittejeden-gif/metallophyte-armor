# Metallophyte-Armor

Metallophyte-Armor is a conceptual plant-biomaterials proposal for capturing copper already present in contaminated soil in monitored, harvestable inner wood.

**Live proposal:** [pinterittejeden-gif.github.io/metallophyte-armor](https://pinterittejeden-gif.github.io/metallophyte-armor/)

## Research question

Can copper move through a pine in a buffered form and become preferentially immobilized during sapwood-to-heartwood transition without unacceptable plant, environmental, processing, or disposal harm?

## Evidence status

- **Established:** plant metal handling, phytoremediation principles, copper toxicology, copper-based wood preservation, and wood anatomy.
- **Proposed:** coupling temporary transport buffering to stronger transition-zone capture.
- **Unknown:** suitable regulatory elements, copper speciation, whole-tree mass balance, trait stability, material benefit, and lifecycle safety.
- **Not claimed:** engineered trees, tested lumber, successful remediation, or architectural readiness.
